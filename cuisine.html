Cuisine

Chicken Udon Soup

A super simple one-pot chicken udon soup that guarantees maximum satisfaction with beautifully seared chicken, rich soup, thick noodles, and tender veggies.

Botchan Dango

Botchan Dango is a traditional Japanese sweet that consists of three mochi (rice flour dumplings) on a bamboo skewer. The dumplings are wrapped in bean paste flavored with red adzuki beans, eggs, and green tea, that give them their three distinctive colors.

Tai Meshi

In the Uwajima region of Ehime, locals enjoy a dish called Tai Meshi (red snapper and rice), which is regarded as the “soul food” of Ehime. Tai Meshi consists of a bowl of cooked rice topped with red snapper sashimi that has been dipped in raw egg yolk mixed with a sweet sauce.

Tokushima Ramen

Tokushima Ramen comes in shades of dark brown, yellow and white, depending on whether pork, chicken or vegetable broth is used, and whether the soy sauce added is dark or light. Bowls of the broth and soft, thin noodles are served topped with slices of seasoned pork ribs, green onions, and a raw egg that cooks in the hot soup.
