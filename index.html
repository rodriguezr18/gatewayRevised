SHIKOKU

Information about Shikoku:

Shikoku, which literally means "four provinces" is the second smallest main island of Japan after Okinawa. Shikoku is encircled by a 1,200km, 88-temple Buddhist pilgrimage route (henro). This route honors the 9th-century monk Kukai. This island is known for its pleasant climate and slow paced way of life. The coast of Shikoku is surrounded by small ports, fishing villages, and salt collecting areas, whilenland, you can find farms situated on the islands gentle-sloping hills and steep mountains. The rivers have incredibly clear water.
